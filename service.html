Community Service

Giving Back is Important...

Which is why I make an effort to give back to my community as much as I can. Apart from the organizations I've included below, I was also a youth group calendar, and frequently tutor peers in a variety of engineering disciplines. Below, I've highlighted the two service organizations that I dedicate the most significant portion of my time to.

Texas A&M National Scholars Ambassadors

TANSA is an on-campus organization focused on recruitment of national scholars to attend Texas A&M. When I was a prospective student, I got to have lunch with a panel of TANSA members, which heavily influenced my decision to attend A&M. I absolutely love my school, and cannot imagine myself being anywhere else. Because of that, I joined TANSA: I want to be able to share my passion for A&M with prospective students, in order to help them get to what I believe is the best college education in America. As an out-of-state student, I was nervous coming into A&M, which is a majority in-state school. However, I cannot imagine myself anywhere else, and love sharing that passion with others.

Chabad On-Campus

Another organization that I spend a lot of time at is the Chabad at Texas A&M. Through Chabad, I have not only been able to connect more strongly with my Jewish identity, but also find a second family away from home. My freshman year, I largely was just a participant. However, now as a sophomore, I have started working at Chabad in order to help cook for ~80 other Jewish Aggies weekly. Although I am not very religious, Chabad has been an incredibly welcoming and inclusive community. From bringing students soup when they get sick to events with Kosher food at least three days a week, it has been a truly positive place in every aspect. If you are curious to see some of the events they host, check out the TAMU Chabad Instagram!
